A Brief Overview of Long Bien Bridge

Long Bien Bridge was once the second longest bridge in the world

Long Bien Bridge is the first steel bridge spanning the Red River, connecting the two districts of Hoan Kiem and Long Bien in Hanoi. The bridge was built by the French from 1898 to 1902 during the time of the then Governor-General of Indochina, Doumer. The bridge is 2,290 meters long across the river and has an additional 896 meters of approach ramps, consisting of 19 steel spans placed on 20 piers with a unique architectural design. The bridge was designed with a single railway track in the middle and two lanes on either side for bicycles and pedestrians. One little-known fact about the bridge is that it was once the second longest bridge in the world (only behind the Brooklyn Bridge spanning the East River in the U.S.), and it was even referred to as the "horizontal Eiffel Tower of Hanoi."

Historical Events Associated with Long Bien Bridge

Long Bien Bridge has witnessed the most important milestones of the nation, including two of our biggest resistance wars: the war against the French colonialists and the war against American imperialism.

  • 1945 Ho Chi Minh read the Declaration of Independence at Ba Dinh Square
  • October 1954 Hanoi was flooded with flags and flowers celebrating the liberation of the capital
  • 1975 Independence

View of Hanoi from Above

Long Bien Bridge

Long Bien Bridge is likely the one associated with many memories. It’s the bridge that young people often ride to, stop at, and enjoy the breeze on a whim or whenever they feel the need for some open space to breathe. The bridge is also a gathering spot for students who come to play music and sing together, a place to hold onto the memories of our youth.

Tran Nhat Duat Café

Tran Nhat Duat Café

Tran Nhat Duat Café, located on the 4th floor with an open space, offers a distant view of Long Bien Bridge. Here, you can enjoy a cup of coffee, chat, and gaze out at the ancient bridge, Red River rock banks, bustling markets, and the vast surroundings. This spot provides a different perspective of Long Bien Bridge, a view from above instead of the usual view from the Red River rock banks below.

Eating Grilled Corn and Sweet Potatoes in Winter

Eating Grilled Corn and Sweet Potatoes

In winter, Long Bien Bridge becomes the perfect place to sit and enjoy grilled corn and sweet potatoes, feeling the chilly wind and warming your hands near the fire. The warm steam from the sweet potatoes will warm your heart immensely.
